Ascending Etiquette — Skyscraper Toilet

Stainless steel, concrete, crowd-control protocol | Hundred-meter scale | Urban intervention

This work raises everyday shame to a metropolitan altitude: a toilet as tall as a tower, tuned to the district's wind and reflecting the weather and signage around it. Visitors ascend a narrow sky-stair, wavering between exposure and ceremony. By erecting the city's hidden plumbing into open air, the artist turns a private need into a public skyline.

Here, queuing is not only waiting—it is a form of civic choreography. Footsteps, breath, patience, and the gaze of bystanders compose a score of public life. You don't have to enter a gallery; you only have to look up and watch the city rinse and recirculate its own order.
